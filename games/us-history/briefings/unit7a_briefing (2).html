Unit 7A: Camelot

Use these briefing slides to study the major ideas, events, and changes of Unit 7A. This section follows the arc from Kennedy's Camelot image to reform, counterculture, and the growing crisis in Vietnam.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores the optimism of the early 1960s, the promises and limits of reform, the rise of cultural rebellion, and the way Cold War tensions helped pull the United States deeper into Vietnam.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did early 1960s optimism give way to reform, protest, conflict, and deeper national division?

What to Notice

  • How image and reality often differed.
  • How Cold War fears shaped decisions.
  • How reform movements pushed the nation to change.
  • How the 1960s became more divided over time.

Study Tip

Do not treat these lessons as isolated topics. Try to see one connected story: Camelot, reform, backlash, protest, and Vietnam all fit into a broader transformation of American life.
