Lesson 15: Through Ellis Island and Angel Island

A student study file on the immigrant experience in industrial America, tracing why people left home, how they arrived, what they faced at Ellis Island and Angel Island, and how opportunity mixed with prejudice, crowded cities, and exclusion.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did immigrants pursue opportunity in the United States while facing inspection, hard work, crowded cities, and rising nativist hostility?

    Key Terms

    push factors
    pull factors
    Ellis Island
    Angel Island
    nativism
    Chinese Exclusion Act
    tenements
    Americanization

    What to Watch For

    1. How poverty, persecution, and limited opportunity pushed people out of their home countries while American jobs and communities pulled them in.
    2. How Ellis Island and Angel Island reflected very different immigration experiences on the East and West Coasts.
    3. How immigrant neighborhoods offered work, culture, and support while also exposing families to crowding and hardship.
    4. How nativism, anti-Chinese violence, and federal restrictions revealed that not all newcomers were welcomed equally.

    Study Tip

    Track the journey in stages: why people left, how they arrived, where they settled, and how Americans reacted to them. If you follow that sequence, the full immigrant experience stays organized and easy to recall.
