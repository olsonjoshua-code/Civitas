Lesson 46: The Widening Struggle for Civil Rights

A student study file on how the civil rights movement expanded in the 1960s and 1970s to include women, Latinos, Indigenous Americans, Asian Americans, Americans with disabilities, LGBT Americans, and older Americans.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why and how did the civil rights movement expand?

    Key Terms

    women's liberation

    AIM

    What to Watch For

    1. How the Black freedom struggle inspired many other groups to organize for equal treatment.
    2. How different groups focused on different issues, such as wages, schools, land, disability access, or identity.
    3. How protest, organizing, court action, and federal legislation all played a role in widening civil rights.
    4. How gains often came with backlash, delay, and unfinished struggles that continue into the present.

    Study Tip

    Do not memorize this lesson as separate mini-stories. See the common pattern: exclusion, organizing, protest, legal change, backlash, and partial victory. That pattern links nearly every movement in the lesson.
