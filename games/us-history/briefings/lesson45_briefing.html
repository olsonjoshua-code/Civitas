    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did civil rights activists change their strategies and goals in the 1960s and 1970s, and how successful were they in achieving racial equality?

    Key Terms

    Black power
    Watts riot
    Kerner Commission
    Nation of Islam
    Black Panther Party
    Civil Rights Act of 1968
    Swann v. Charlotte-Mecklenburg
    affirmative action

    What to Watch For

    1. How frustration over slow progress pushed some activists away from nonviolence and integration toward Black power and self-determination.
    2. How riots, poverty, housing discrimination, and police brutality exposed the unfinished work of civil rights.
    3. How Black political and cultural pride grew through Afrocentrism, Kwanzaa, elections, and new community-based activism.
    4. How the federal government confronted racism through fair housing laws, busing, and affirmative action, while also creating new controversies.

    Study Tip

    This lesson is about change inside the civil rights movement. Do not just memorize names. Track how goals shifted from integration and nonviolence toward community control, Black pride, political power, and economic opportunity.
