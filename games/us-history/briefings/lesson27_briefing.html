Lesson 27: Normalcy

A student study file on how Harding, Coolidge, and Hoover helped define the politics of normalcy through pro-business government, limited reform, selective diplomacy, and confidence in prosperity, even as scandal and speculation exposed the limits of that calm.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did the politics of normalcy appeal to so many Americans in the 1920s, and what did they reveal about business power, government priorities, and the limits of prosperity?

    Key Terms

    normalcy
    Warren G. Harding
    Calvin Coolidge
    Herbert Hoover
    Teapot Dome
    Dawes Plan
    speculation
    Florida land boom

    What to Watch For

    1. How the election of 1920 and Harding's slogan of normalcy reflected voter exhaustion after war, reform, and unrest.
    2. How Harding, Coolidge, and Hoover promoted low taxes, light regulation, and closer ties between government and business.
    3. How Republican leaders avoided Wilsonian internationalism while still shaping world affairs through conferences and financial plans.
    4. How scandal, speculation, and uneven prosperity revealed weaknesses beneath the image of calm and success.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson in three parts: why voters wanted normalcy, how leaders translated that into policy, and where the promises of prosperity started to break down. That pattern keeps the politics organized.
