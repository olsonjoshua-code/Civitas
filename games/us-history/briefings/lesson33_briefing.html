A student study file on Roosevelt's response to the Great Depression, tracing the First and Second New Deals, political challenges from left and right, labor rights, Social Security, the court-packing fight, and the lasting transformation of the federal government's role in American life.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did the expansion of government during the New Deal change the relationship between Americans and their federal government — and did those changes go far enough?

    Key Terms

    AAA
    CCC
    NIRA / NRA
    TVA
    WPA
    Wagner Act
    Social Security Act
    New Deal Coalition

    What to Watch For

    1. How FDR used fireside chats and personal style to restore public trust before any programs were even passed.
    2. How the First New Deal targeted banks, businesses, farmers, and the unemployed — and why critics from both left and right found it insufficient.
    3. How the Second New Deal shifted from relief toward long-term reform, producing the Wagner Act, WPA, and Social Security.
    4. How the New Deal's benefits were uneven — better for workers and the elderly than for African Americans, women, or Mexican Americans.

    Study Tip

    Organize the New Deal into three questions: Who did it help? Who did it miss? What did it permanently change? That framework will hold the whole lesson together and help you evaluate its legacy.
