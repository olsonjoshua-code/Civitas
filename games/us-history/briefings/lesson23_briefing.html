    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How was World War I fought, why did it become so deadly, and how did American troops help shape the final outcome?

    Key Terms

    trench warfare
    attrition
    machine gun
    U-boat
    AEF
    John J. Pershing
    369th Infantry
    Meuse-Argonne

    What to Watch For

    1. How trench systems, defensive weapons, and attrition turned the war into a long industrial struggle.
    2. How new technology such as machine guns, submarines, and aircraft changed the conduct of battle.
    3. How the American Expeditionary Force entered the war and why Pershing wanted U.S. troops to fight as a distinct force.
    4. How the 369th Infantry, the Meuse-Argonne Offensive, and the casualty totals reveal the human cost of the war.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson in three parts: how the war was fought, how the United States entered the battlefield, and what the final offensives cost. That pattern keeps the whole lesson organized.
