Lesson 56: Ending the Cold War

A student study file on the Cold War's final phase: Reagan's pressure, Gorbachev's reforms, the fall of the Berlin Wall, the Soviet collapse, and the new uncertainties that followed.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did pressure, reform, protest, and political crisis bring the Cold War to an end, and what uncertainties followed?

    Key Terms

    Cold War
    Gorbachev
    Glasnost
    Perestroika
    Berlin Wall
    Tiananmen Square
    Iran-Contra Affair
    Boris Yeltsin

    What to Watch For

    1. How Reagan used both military pressure and diplomacy in dealing with the Soviet Union.
    2. How Gorbachev's reforms weakened communist control instead of saving it.
    3. How the end of the Cold War mixed hope with violence, scandal, and unfinished struggles.
    4. How the Soviet collapse left the United States facing a very different world rather than a fully peaceful one.

    Study Tip

    Split the lesson into two tracks: why the Soviet system weakened and what the United States was doing at the same time. Once those stories meet, the end of the Cold War makes much more sense.
