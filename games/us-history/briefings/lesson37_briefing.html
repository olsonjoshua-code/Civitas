Lesson 37: Aftermath of Victory

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did victory in World War II not produce a simple peace, and how did postwar decisions reshape American society, global leadership, and the emerging world order?

    Key Terms

    V-J Day
    demobilization
    GI Bill
    Four Freedoms
    United Nations
    Nuremberg Trials
    occupation
    superpowers
    Cold War

    What to Watch For

    1. How celebration at the war’s end quickly gave way to difficult questions about jobs, veterans, and postwar adjustment.
    2. How the GI Bill and women’s wartime labor changed American society after 1945.
    3. How the United Nations and the Four Freedoms reflected hopes for a more stable international order.
    4. How Nuremberg, occupation policy, and rising U.S.-Soviet tension showed that victory did not bring a simple peace.

    Study Tip

    Follow the lesson as a transition story: celebration, reintegration at home, new opportunities and tensions, international rebuilding, and the first signs of a new rivalry. That sequence makes the aftermath easier to track.
