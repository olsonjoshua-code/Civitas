    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How have recent presidents tried to fulfill their domestic policy goals in an era of close elections, polarization, and gridlock?

    Key Terms

    New Democrat
    Contract with America
    Bush v. Gore
    No Child Left Behind
    Great Recession
    Stimulus
    Debt ceiling
    Gridlock

    What to Watch For

    1. How red-state blue-state politics, independents, and third parties reshaped elections after 1992.
    2. How Clinton, Bush, Obama, and Trump all had major goals but ran into divided government or scandal.
    3. How crises like Katrina, the housing crash, COVID-19, and January 6 affected public trust in government.
    4. How the Supreme Court and Congress became just as important as presidents in shaping domestic politics.

    Study Tip

    Organize the lesson by presidency. For each president, ask three quick questions: what was the goal, what actually passed, and what blocked or damaged that agenda. That makes the long timeline much easier to follow.
