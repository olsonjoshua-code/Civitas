    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did the fight over the Treaty of Versailles reveal competing visions of peace, presidential leadership, and the United States' future role in world affairs?

    Key Terms

    Treaty of Versailles
    Fourteen Points
    League of Nations
    Henry Cabot Lodge
    reservationists
    irreconcilables
    Article X
    ratification

    What to Watch For

    1. How Wilson's Fourteen Points offered an idealistic peace, and how the final treaty reflected harsher Allied priorities.
    2. How the Paris Peace Conference and the redrawing of Europe changed the postwar world.
    3. How Senate critics, especially Henry Cabot Lodge, challenged the League of Nations and Article X.
    4. How Wilson's speaking tour, stroke, and refusal to compromise helped lead to the treaty's defeat in the Senate.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson in three stages: Wilson's peace vision, the treaty the Allies actually produced, and the ratification fight back in the United States. That sequence makes the debate easier to follow.
