Unit 4B: Depression and New Deal America

Use these briefing slides to study the collapse of the American economy, the government’s response to crisis, the daily hardships faced by ordinary Americans, and the long-term impact of the New Deal on American life and government.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores what caused the Great Depression, how different leaders and ordinary Americans responded to the crisis, and how Franklin Roosevelt’s New Deal transformed the relationship between citizens and the federal government.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

What caused the Great Depression, how did Americans respond to it, and how did the New Deal reshape government and daily life?

What to Notice

  • How weaknesses in the 1920s economy made the crash more devastating.
  • How Americans disagreed over whether government should intervene and how much.
  • How the Depression affected ordinary people in different ways across cities, farms, and regions.
  • How New Deal programs expanded federal power and created a lasting political legacy.

Study Tip

Follow the unit as a chain of cause and response: first identify why the economy collapsed, then examine how people suffered, then study how Hoover and Roosevelt responded, and finally ask how much the New Deal permanently changed America.
