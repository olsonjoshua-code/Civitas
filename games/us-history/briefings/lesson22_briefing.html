    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did the United States try to stay neutral in World War I at first, and what finally pushed it into war in 1917?

    Key Terms

    neutrality
    alliances
    trench warfare
    stalemate
    Lusitania
    preparedness
    Zimmermann Telegram
    unrestricted submarine warfare

    What to Watch For

    1. How Europe’s alliance system turned a regional crisis into a continent-wide war.
    2. How trench warfare and stalemate made the conflict longer, deadlier, and harder for Americans to ignore.
    3. How submarine warfare, the Lusitania, and the Zimmermann note undermined neutrality.
    4. How Wilson moved from neutrality and preparedness to asking Congress for war in 1917.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson in four steps: causes in Europe, deadlock on the battlefield, pressure on American neutrality, and the final decision for war. That sequence makes the shift much easier to follow.
