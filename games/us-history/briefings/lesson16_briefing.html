    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    What problems did rapid industrial and urban growth create at the turn of the century, and why did reformers believe those problems could no longer be ignored?

    Key Terms

    urbanization
    tenements
    political machine
    muckraker
    monopoly
    consumer protection
    child labor
    segregation

    What to Watch For

    1. How population growth, migration, and industrial concentration changed where Americans lived and worked.
    2. How city life produced crowding, machine politics, poverty, unsafe workplaces, and weak consumer protections.
    3. How corporate wealth and political influence raised fears that democracy was being overwhelmed by private power.
    4. How racial injustice and uneven access to modern comforts showed that progress was not shared equally.

    Study Tip

    Sort the lesson into four buckets: population change, city problems, economic power, and inequality. If you can place each slide into one of those groups, the logic of Progressive reform becomes much easier to follow.
