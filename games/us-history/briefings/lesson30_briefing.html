    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    What caused the most severe economic crisis in American history, and how did individual choices, structural problems, and government decisions combine to deepen it?

    Key Terms

    Black Tuesday
    buying on margin
    overproduction
    underconsumption
    bank run
    Federal Reserve
    Hawley-Smoot Tariff
    speculative bubble

    What to Watch For

    1. How speculative stock buying in the 1920s created a fragile market with no solid foundation.
    2. How the stock market crash triggered a cascade of bank failures that wiped out ordinary Americans' savings.
    3. How overproduction and underconsumption had already been weakening the economy before 1929.
    4. How government decisions — rising interest rates and high tariffs — made a bad situation dramatically worse.

    Study Tip

    Don't think of the Depression as a single event. Track it as a chain reaction: stock speculation → crash → bank failures → lost savings → reduced spending → layoffs → more lost spending. Each link made the next one worse.
