    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did frustration with the Vietnam War grow so sharply by 1968?

    Key Terms

    Guerrilla Warfare
    Search-and-Destroy
    Booby Traps
    Tunnel Networks
    Tet Offensive
    Credibility Gap
    Public Opinion
    Antiwar Protest

    What to Watch For

    1. How guerrilla tactics and terrain undercut American military advantages.
    2. Why tunnels, traps, and elusive enemies made victory hard to measure.
    3. How television coverage and Tet widened the credibility gap at home.
    4. Why military frustration turned into political crisis for Johnson and the Democrats.

    Study Tip

    Split the lesson into two halves: battlefield frustration and political frustration. Once you connect those two stories, the rise of protest and Johnson's collapse make much more sense.
