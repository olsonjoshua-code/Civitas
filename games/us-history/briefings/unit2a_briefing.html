Unit 2A: Expansion, Industry, and Immigration

Use these briefing slides to study the major transformations of the late 1800s and early 1900s. This unit follows westward expansion, the rise of industry and big business, labor’s response to industrialization, and the experiences of immigrants entering a rapidly changing nation.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores how the United States expanded west, industrialized at a rapid pace, attracted millions of immigrants, and experienced growing conflict over labor, opportunity, equality, and who truly benefited from national growth.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did westward expansion, industrial growth, labor conflict, and immigration transform the United States in the late 1800s?

What to Notice

  • How expansion created both opportunity and conflict in the West.
  • How new inventions and business systems changed American life.
  • How industrial growth created wealth but also dangerous working conditions.
  • How immigrants were drawn by opportunity but often faced suspicion, hardship, and exclusion.

Study Tip

Think of this unit as one connected story: railroads, industry, labor, and immigration all fed into each other. As the nation grew more modern and powerful, debates over fairness, freedom, work, and belonging became even more intense.
