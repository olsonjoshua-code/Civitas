    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How well have U.S. foreign policy decisions met the challenges of the global age?

    Key Terms

    globalization
    European Union
    humanitarian aid
    al Qaeda
    war on terror
    Geneva Conventions
    DHS
    Islamic State

    What to Watch For

    1. How globalization after the Cold War linked trade, technology, disease, and security more tightly than ever.
    2. How the United States responded differently to genocide, refugee crises, famine, and natural disasters.
    3. How 9/11 reshaped policy through Afghanistan, Iraq, Guantanamo, DHS, and surveillance.
    4. How Russia, ISIS, cyberattacks, China, and regional instability kept foreign policy unsettled.

    Study Tip

    Break the chapter into four buckets: globalization, genocide and aid, the war on terror, and newer global threats. That makes it easier to see how very different events still fit the same foreign-policy story.
