Lesson 9: A Dividing Nation

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did repeated efforts to compromise over slavery fail to keep the nation together?

    Key Terms

    Missouri Compromise
    Compromise of 1850
    Kansas-Nebraska Act
    popular sovereignty
    Dred Scott
    Uncle Tom's Cabin
    John Brown
    Fort Sumter

    What to Watch For

    1. How each compromise over slavery calmed one crisis but made the next one worse.
    2. How books, speeches, court rulings, and violence all deepened sectional hatred.
    3. How antislavery resistance and proslavery reaction pushed moderates toward harder positions.
    4. How Lincoln's election and the attack on Fort Sumter turned division into civil war.

    Study Tip

    Read the lesson like a chain reaction: compromise, backlash, violence, court ruling, secession, war. If you track how one event leads to the next, the whole prewar crisis becomes much easier to remember.
