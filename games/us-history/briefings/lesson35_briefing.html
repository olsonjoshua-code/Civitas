Lesson 35: The Impact of World War II on Americans

A student study file tracing how World War II reshaped American life at home and abroad, from wartime production and rationing to the service of women, African Americans, Mexican Americans, and Japanese Americans.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    What kinds of opportunities and hardships did the war create for Americans at home and abroad?

    Key Terms

    Double V campaign
    Executive Order 9066
    Korematsu v. U.S.
    Tuskegee Airmen
    War Production Board
    War Refugee Board
    Women's Army Corps
    Zoot Suit Riots

    What to Watch For

    1. How the War Production Board transformed American industry to meet military demands.
    2. How women, African Americans, and Mexican Americans found new roles on the home front and in uniform.
    3. How Japanese Americans lost their civil rights through internment despite their loyalty.
    4. How Jewish Americans responded to the persecution of European Jews and the limits of U.S. policy.

    Study Tip

    Track two themes at once: how the war created new opportunities for Americans, and how it deepened existing inequalities. The same conflict that opened factory doors also put Japanese Americans behind barbed wire.
