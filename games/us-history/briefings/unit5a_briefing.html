Unit 5A: World War II and the Postwar Order

Use these briefing slides to study how World War II began, how Americans experienced the war at home and abroad, how the Allies fought to defeat the Axis powers, and how the United States helped shape the postwar world.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores the origins of World War II, the opportunities and hardships the war created for Americans, the military strategies used to defeat the Axis powers, and the major political, economic, and moral questions that shaped the postwar order.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did World War II transform the United States, and how did the nation help shape the postwar world that followed?

What to Notice

  • How the failures of appeasement and collective security contributed to war.
  • How wartime mobilization created both opportunity and injustice on the home front.
  • How Allied strategy differed in Europe and the Pacific.
  • How the United States moved away from isolationism and toward global leadership after the war.

Study Tip

Track the unit as one connected arc: first the world failed to stop aggression, then the United States mobilized for total war, then the Allies defeated the Axis, and finally American leaders tried to build a more stable postwar order than the one that followed World War I.
