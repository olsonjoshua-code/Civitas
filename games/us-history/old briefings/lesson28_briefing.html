Lesson 28: The Jazz Age

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did new technology, consumer culture, and urban creativity turn the 1920s into the Jazz Age, and why did that popular culture change how Americans thought about entertainment, identity, and modern life?

    Key Terms

    mass media
    consumer culture
    installment buying
    Jazz Age
    Harlem Renaissance
    modernism
    celebrity culture
    flapper

    What to Watch For

    1. How radio, movies, and advertising created shared national experiences and helped build a true mass audience.
    2. How cars, dance, fashion, and city nightlife reshaped daily behavior, leisure, and youth culture.
    3. How African American musicians, writers, and artists stood at the center of the Jazz Age and the Harlem Renaissance.
    4. How athletes, pilots, writers, and reformers became celebrities who redefined fame, gender roles, and modern identity.

    Study Tip

    Group the lesson into four lanes: media, consumer life, arts, and famous public figures. That makes it easier to see how different parts of the decade combined into one larger modern mass culture.
