    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did the anxieties raised by the Cold War affect life in the United States?

    Key Terms

    HUAC
    McCarthyism
    Rosenberg trial
    Alger Hiss case
    Atomic Age
    FCDA
    blacklist
    duck-and-cover

    What to Watch For

    1. How HUAC and loyalty oaths brought Cold War suspicion into everyday American life.
    2. How spy cases like the Rosenbergs and Alger Hiss deepened fears of communist infiltration.
    3. How McCarthy rose to power on unproven accusations — and how he fell when he went too far.
    4. How the Atomic Age created both wonder and terror, and how civil defense tried to manage the unmanageable.

    Study Tip

    Follow two parallel stories at once: the political fear of communists within (HUAC, McCarthy, spy trials) and the physical fear of nuclear attack (duck-and-cover, fallout shelters, FCDA). Both reflect the same underlying anxiety — that America was under threat from all directions.
