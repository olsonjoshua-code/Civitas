    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did civil rights activists advance the ideals of liberty, equality, and opportunity for African Americans?

    Key Terms

    Civil Rights Act of 1964
    Freedom Rides
    Freedom Summer
    March on Washington
    Montgomery Bus Boycott
    SCLC
    SNCC
    Voting Rights Act of 1965

    What to Watch For

    1. How nonviolent direct action created pressure that peaceful protest alone could not.
    2. How each confrontation — Birmingham, Freedom Rides, Selma — built public support for federal legislation.
    3. How television transformed local protests into national crises.
    4. How the Civil Rights and Voting Rights Acts changed the legal landscape of American life.

    Study Tip

    Follow the movement as an escalating sequence: local boycotts led to legal victories, which exposed remaining barriers, which demanded larger campaigns, which finally forced Congress to act. Each step built on the last.
