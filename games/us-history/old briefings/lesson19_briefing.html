Lesson 19: Foreign Policy: Setting a Course of Expansionism

A student study file on how the United States moved from early caution in foreign affairs toward continental and Pacific expansion through the War of 1812, the Monroe Doctrine, westward growth, war with Mexico, and opening trade in Asia.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did U.S. foreign policy shift from avoiding entanglements to pursuing continental and overseas expansion?

    Key Terms

    Farewell Address
    War of 1812
    Louisiana Purchase
    Monroe Doctrine
    Manifest Destiny
    Alamo
    Guadalupe Hidalgo
    Commodore Perry

    What to Watch For

    1. How early U.S. leaders first warned against deep foreign entanglements while still defending national independence.
    2. How westward growth and Manifest Destiny pushed foreign policy toward continental expansion.
    3. How the Monroe Doctrine and war with Mexico increased U.S. confidence and territorial reach.
    4. How trade with Japan and competition in Samoa signaled a growing American role in the Pacific.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson by direction of change: early caution, continental expansion, hemispheric influence, and then Pacific outreach. That sequence makes the whole foreign-policy story easier to follow.
