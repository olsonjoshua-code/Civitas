Lesson 12: Change and Conflict in the American West

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did western expansion create opportunity for some Americans while bringing conflict, exploitation, and loss to others?

    Key Terms

    westward migration
    Exodusters
    transcontinental railroad
    Chinese laborers
    cowboys
    Grange
    Populism
    assimilation

    What to Watch For

    1. How railroads, land hunger, and mining pulled people west and tied the region into a national market.
    2. How different western groups experienced expansion very differently: settlers, miners, ranchers, farmers, Native peoples, and migrants.
    3. How technology and debt reshaped farming on the Great Plains.
    4. How Native land loss, resistance, and federal assimilation policy were central parts of western history.

    Study Tip

    Don’t picture the West as just cowboys and open land. Track three forces together: transportation, migration, and conflict over land. That’s what ties the whole lesson together.
