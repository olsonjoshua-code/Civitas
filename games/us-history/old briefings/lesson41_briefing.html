Lesson 41: Peace, Prosperity, and Progress

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did the years after World War II bring both rapid prosperity and major social change, and why did growth, suburbs, consumer culture, and new technology reshape daily life in the United States?

    Key Terms

    gross national product
    inflation
    consumerism
    planned obsolescence
    suburbs
    baby boom
    Sun Belt
    white-collar work
    Jonas Salk

    What to Watch For

    1. How wartime savings and pent-up demand helped launch a powerful postwar boom.
    2. How inflation and strikes showed that prosperity did not arrive without tension.
    3. How cars, shopping centers, and advertising fueled a culture of consumer buying.
    4. How suburbs, the baby boom, and Sun Belt migration changed where and how Americans lived.
    5. How medical breakthroughs like the polio vaccine made the era feel like a time of progress.

    Study Tip

    Split the lesson into two tracks. Track the economic changes first: output, prices, jobs, cars, and housing. Then track the social changes: family life, suburbs, Sun Belt growth, and medical progress. Putting those two tracks together makes the whole lesson much easier to remember.
