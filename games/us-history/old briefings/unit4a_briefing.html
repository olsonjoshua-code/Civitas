Unit 4A: The Roaring Twenties

Use these briefing slides to study the tensions, politics, popular culture, and cultural conflicts that shaped the United States in the 1920s. This unit follows the uneasy aftermath of World War I, the Republican call for normalcy, the rise of consumer culture, and the clash between traditional and modern values.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores how the 1920s brought prosperity, cultural change, and new forms of leisure for some Americans while also deepening tensions over immigration, race, civil liberties, religion, gender roles, and the meaning of American identity.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did the prosperity and cultural change of the 1920s also produce conflict over identity, values, and America’s founding ideals?

What to Notice

  • How fear and instability after World War I fueled nativism and suspicion.
  • How Republican leadership emphasized normalcy, business growth, and limited government intervention.
  • How consumer culture and mass media changed daily life and popular entertainment.
  • How the decade’s modern trends sparked backlash from traditionalists in religion, morality, and small-town America.

Study Tip

Do not treat the 1920s as just a fun decade of jazz and prosperity. Track both sides at once: rising wealth and culture on one side, fear, exclusion, and deep value conflicts on the other. That tension is the heart of the unit.
