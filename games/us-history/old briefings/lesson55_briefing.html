    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did Ronald Reagan and the conservative movement shift American politics to the right during the 1980s?

    Key Terms

    Reagan Revolution
    Supply-side economics
    Deregulation
    Religious Right
    PATCO
    Sandra Day O'Connor
    Budget deficit
    AIDS crisis

    What to Watch For

    1. How frustration with the 1970s opened the door for Reagan in 1980.
    2. How tax cuts, deregulation, and a hard line toward labor helped define Reaganomics.
    3. How social conservatism, judicial appointments, and political messaging broadened the conservative coalition.
    4. How critics pointed to inequality, deficits, and the slow response to AIDS as limits of the Reagan era.

    Study Tip

    Break the lesson into four parts: why Reagan won, what Reagan changed, who pushed back, and what legacy remained. That makes the "shift to the right" feel like a process instead of just an election result.
