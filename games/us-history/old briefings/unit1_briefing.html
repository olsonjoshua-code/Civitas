Unit 1: A Nation Divided

Use these briefing slides to study the major ideas, conflicts, and changes of this unit. This section follows the growing sectional crisis, the Civil War itself, and the struggle to rebuild the nation during Reconstruction.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores how sectional conflict over slavery pushed the United States toward war, how the Civil War transformed the nation, and how Reconstruction tested whether the country would truly extend liberty, equality, and citizenship after slavery ended.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did conflict over slavery break apart the Union, transform the nation through war, and test America’s founding ideals during Reconstruction?

What to Notice

  • How sectionalism deepened political conflict.
  • How the Civil War changed from a fight for union into a struggle over freedom.
  • How Reconstruction expanded rights on paper but faced resistance in practice.
  • How debates over citizenship, equality, and federal power continued after the war.

Study Tip

Do not study these as three separate topics. See the chain of cause and effect: sectional division led to war, war destroyed slavery, and Reconstruction asked whether the nation would actually live up to its ideals afterward.
