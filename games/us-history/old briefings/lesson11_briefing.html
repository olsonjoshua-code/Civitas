    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did Reconstruction open new possibilities for freedom and citizenship but fail to protect them permanently?

    Key Terms

    Reconstruction
    Freedmen's Bureau
    Radical Republicans
    Andrew Johnson
    military districts
    sharecropping
    carpetbaggers

    What to Watch For

    1. How rebuilding the South meant deciding who held power, land, rights, and protection.
    2. How Black freedom expanded through schools, political office, and federal policy.
    3. How Johnson and Congress fought over the meaning and pace of Reconstruction.
    4. How violence, economic dependence, and segregation undercut Reconstruction's gains.

    Study Tip

    Track Reconstruction through two competing stories at once: major advances in Black citizenship and the determined effort to roll those gains back. That tension is the key to the whole lesson.
