    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did the Civil War become both a fight to save the Union and a struggle over the future of freedom?

    Key Terms

    Union
    Confederacy
    Bull Run

    conscription

    What to Watch For

    1. How the Union's larger population, industry, and railroads gave it long-term advantages.
    2. How early battles shattered hopes for a short and easy war.
    3. How emancipation and Black military service changed the meaning of the conflict.
    4. How civilian hardship, riots, medicine, and total war made the Civil War a whole-society struggle.

    Study Tip

    Track the war through three threads at the same time: battlefield turning points, home-front pressures, and the growth of emancipation. That will keep the lesson from feeling like just a list of battles.
