    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why can the 1920s be described as a conflict between tradition and modernity, and how did debates over prohibition, science, religion, women’s behavior, and city culture reveal deeper arguments about American identity?

    Key Terms

    traditionalism
    modernism
    Scopes Trial
    Prohibition
    bootlegging
    flapper
    rural-urban divide
    fundamentalism

    What to Watch For

    1. How city life, mass entertainment, and new social freedoms made modernism feel exciting to many Americans and threatening to others.
    2. How Prohibition exposed the gap between moral reform ideals and the realities of urban resistance, bootlegging, and weak enforcement.
    3. How the Scopes Trial turned a local classroom dispute into a national fight over science, religion, and cultural authority.
    4. How changing roles for women, shifting fashions, and public behavior became visible symbols of the larger culture war.

    Study Tip

    Sort the slides into three tensions: city versus country, science versus tradition, and freedom versus control. That structure makes the whole lesson easier to remember because most of the 1920s conflicts fit inside one of those debates.
