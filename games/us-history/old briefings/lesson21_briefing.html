    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did the United States try to manage its new global power after 1898, and what tensions did that create between empire and democratic ideals?

    Key Terms

    protectorate
    Open Door Policy
    Panama Canal
    Roosevelt Corollary
    Big Stick diplomacy
    dollar diplomacy
    moral diplomacy
    Emilio Aguinaldo

    What to Watch For

    1. How new territories such as Puerto Rico and the Philippines forced the United States to decide what empire would look like in practice.
    2. How the Open Door Policy and the Panama Canal expanded American influence through trade, strategy, and infrastructure.
    3. How Roosevelt, Taft, and Wilson used different language and tools to project power abroad.
    4. How foreign-policy success often came with debates over intervention, self-government, and the limits of American ideals.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson by presidency and by method: Roosevelt used the big stick, Taft leaned on finance, and Wilson spoke morally while still intervening. That pattern keeps the whole capstone organized.
