Unit 3B: World War I and Its Consequences

Use these briefing slides to study how the United States moved from neutrality into World War I, how Americans experienced the war abroad and at home, and how the conflict ended in a fierce debate over peace and America’s role in the world.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores why the United States entered World War I, how the war changed warfare and American society, and how the postwar settlement raised lasting questions about democracy, free speech, internationalism, and the nation’s place in global affairs.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did World War I transform the United States politically, socially, and globally, and why did peace prove so difficult to secure afterward?

What to Notice

  • How neutrality became harder to maintain as the war intensified.
  • How World War I introduced new forms of warfare and large-scale mobilization.
  • How the war expanded federal power at home while also restricting dissent.
  • How debates over the Treaty of Versailles reflected deeper disagreements about America’s global role.

Study Tip

Follow the unit as a sequence: first the United States debated entering the war, then it fought and mobilized on a huge scale, and finally it confronted the consequences in the peace settlement. Track how each stage changed the country.
