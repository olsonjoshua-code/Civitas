    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did segregation affect American life in the postwar period?

    Key Terms

    Brown v. Board of Education
    CORE
    de facto segregation
    de jure segregation
    Executive Order 9981
    Warren Court
    NAACP
    Thurgood Marshall

    What to Watch For

    1. How segregation shaped schools, housing, work, marriage, politics, and public accommodations after World War II.
    2. How de jure segregation in the South differed from de facto segregation elsewhere, even while both limited Black opportunity.
    3. How early breakthroughs in sports, the military, and the courts opened small but important cracks in Jim Crow.
    4. How Brown v. Board challenged the legal basis of segregation but did not end resistance or inequality overnight.

    Study Tip

    Do not treat Brown as the beginning of the whole story. Start with how segregation worked in daily life, then follow the small steps toward equality, and finally see Brown as the major legal turning point that helped launch the modern civil rights movement.
