Lesson 24: The Home Front

A student study file on how fighting overseas reshaped life at home through propaganda, food conservation, bond drives, expanded work roles, volunteer service, civil-liberties restrictions, and debates over loyalty and dissent.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did World War I transform life inside the United States, and what tensions did wartime mobilization create on the home front?

    Key Terms

    Liberty Bonds
    Food Administration
    Committee on Public Information
    Four-Minute Men
    Espionage Act
    Sedition Act
    Great Migration
    Red Cross

    What to Watch For

    1. How the federal government shaped food use, borrowing, labor, and public opinion to support the war effort.
    2. How propaganda and the Four-Minute Men encouraged loyalty while criticism of the war became more dangerous.
    3. How women, immigrants, African Americans, and volunteers took on larger public roles during wartime.
    4. How the war expanded participation at home while also creating tensions over free speech, race, and citizenship.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson in three parts: how the government mobilized society, how ordinary people were asked to contribute, and how dissent and inequality persisted underneath wartime unity. That pattern keeps the home-front story organized.
