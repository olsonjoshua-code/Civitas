    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did new technology and big business transform the United States while concentrating wealth and power in fewer hands?

    Key Terms

    Second Industrial Revolution
    Bessemer process
    vertical integration
    horizontal integration
    trust
    laissez-faire
    philanthropy
    assembly line

    What to Watch For

    1. How resources, railroads, research, and electricity accelerated industrial growth after the Civil War.
    2. How business strategies such as trusts and integration helped a few firms dominate entire industries.
    3. How mass production and new inventions changed both factory work and everyday life.
    4. How industrial wealth inspired both admiration for progress and criticism of inequality and monopoly power.

    Study Tip

    Track each slide with three questions in mind: What changed technologically? How did it change production? Who gained the most power, comfort, or profit from that change? That will tie the whole lesson together.
