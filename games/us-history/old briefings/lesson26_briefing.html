    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did the years immediately after World War I feel so unstable, and how did fear shape American responses to labor unrest, race conflict, immigration, and radicalism?

    Key Terms

    First Red Scare
    Palmer Raids
    Red Summer
    Great Migration
    nativism
    quota laws
    Marcus Garvey
    Sacco and Vanzetti

    What to Watch For

    1. How the shift from wartime production to peacetime economy helped produce layoffs, inflation, and labor conflict.
    2. How strikes, bomb scares, and the Russian Revolution fed the First Red Scare and the Palmer Raids.
    3. How racial violence, Black activism, the revived Klan, and anti-Catholic nativism revealed a broader backlash.
    4. How immigration quotas and the Sacco-Vanzetti case showed fear shaping policy, justice, and ideas of belonging.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson in four strands: economic disruption, labor unrest, race and nativism, and political fear. If you sort each example into one of those groups, the postwar tensions become much easier to explain.
