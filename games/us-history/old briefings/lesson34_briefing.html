    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did aggressive dictators, appeasement, and widening Axis expansion turn the 1930s crisis into World War II and eventually pull the United States into the conflict?

    Key Terms

    Adolf Hitler
    Benito Mussolini
    Hideki Tojo
    appeasement
    Munich Conference
    blitzkrieg
    Neutrality Acts
    Lend-Lease
    Pearl Harbor

    What to Watch For

    1. How economic instability and anger after World War I helped dictators gain support in Europe and Asia.
    2. Why Britain and France chose appeasement and how that strategy encouraged more aggression.
    3. How blitzkrieg and Axis expansion transformed regional crises into a wider world war.
    4. How U.S. policy shifted from neutrality to aid and then to direct war after Pearl Harbor.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson as a chain reaction: dictators rise, democracies hesitate, Axis aggression spreads, the war widens, and Pearl Harbor ends the American debate over staying out. That sequence keeps the turning points clear.
