Unit 7B: Vietnam & Watergate

Use these briefing slides to study the major ideas, events, and turning points of the late 1960s and 1970s. This unit follows the deepening frustration of Vietnam, the difficult American withdrawal, Nixon's rise and fall, and the political and social uncertainty of the 1970s.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit traces how Cold War pressure, antiwar protest, executive power, political scandal, and economic anxiety reshaped the United States. It moves from the frustration of Vietnam to the breakdown of trust in government and the unsettled atmosphere of the "Me Decade."

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did war, scandal, and economic uncertainty reshape Americans' trust in leadership and their understanding of the nation's role in the world?

What to Notice

  • How Vietnam weakened confidence in military and political leadership.
  • How antiwar protest and media exposure changed public opinion.
  • How Nixon combined major foreign policy achievements with political self-destruction.
  • How the 1970s mixed reform, anxiety, and a search for new national direction.

Study Tip

Do not treat these lessons as separate topics. Try to see the arc: Vietnam frustration, withdrawal, Nixon's presidency, and 1970s malaise all connect to one larger story about declining trust, contested power, and a changing America.
