Unit 2B: The Progressive Era

Use these briefing slides to study the major problems and reforms of the early 1900s. This unit follows the social, political, and environmental problems Americans confronted at the turn of the century, the reformers who tried to address them, and the way progressive ideas reached the national stage.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores how Americans identified major problems created by industrialization and urban growth, how progressives tried to reform society and government, and how national leaders used federal power to advance progressive goals.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did Americans identify the problems of industrial society, and how effectively did progressives try to solve them?

What to Notice

  • How muckrakers exposed conditions in cities, politics, and industry.
  • How progressive reformers believed government should play a more active role.
  • How different reformers focused on different problems, from child labor to political corruption to public health.
  • How progressive goals moved from local reform efforts to national presidential policy.

Study Tip

See this unit as a sequence: first Americans uncovered serious problems, then reformers organized responses, and finally presidents tried to turn those ideas into national policy. Follow that progression from exposure to reform to federal action.
