    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How could the United States enjoy remarkable prosperity after World War II while millions of Americans still lived in poverty, and why did those inequalities remain hidden from much of the country?

    Key Terms

    poverty
    distribution of wealth
    Appalachia
    working poor
    racial inequality
    Michael Harrington
    The Other America

    What to Watch For

    1. How national prosperity could still leave poor families behind.
    2. Why poverty persisted in Appalachia and other overlooked regions.
    3. How racial discrimination kept many African Americans from sharing in prosperity.
    4. What income data revealed about the uneven distribution of wealth.
    5. Why Michael Harrington challenged the myth that everyone was doing well.

    Study Tip

    Keep asking one question as you move through the lesson: who benefited from postwar prosperity, and who did not? If you track that contrast in each example, the whole lesson becomes much easier to remember.
