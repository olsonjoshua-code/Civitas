Unit 5B: The Early Cold War

Use these briefing slides to study how wartime allies became bitter rivals, how Cold War conflict spread across Europe and Asia, and how fear of communism and nuclear war reshaped life inside the United States.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores how the United States and the Soviet Union moved from uneasy cooperation to open rivalry, how containment shaped American action abroad, and how Cold War anxieties influenced politics, civil liberties, and everyday life at home.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did the Cold War shape American foreign policy abroad and create fear, conformity, and tension at home?

What to Notice

  • How wartime alliance quickly gave way to distrust between the United States and the Soviet Union.
  • How containment pushed the United States into new alliances, interventions, and global commitments.
  • How Europe and Asia became major battlegrounds in the early Cold War.
  • How anti-communist fear at home affected civil liberties, public culture, and everyday behavior.

Study Tip

Track the Cold War on two levels at once: abroad, the United States tried to contain communism through alliances and intervention; at home, fear of subversion and nuclear war shaped politics, schools, and daily life. Both sides of that story matter.
