Lesson 20: The Spanish-American War

A student study file on how events in Cuba, sensational journalism, and the explosion of the USS Maine pushed the United States into war with Spain, and how a short conflict turned into a major turning point in American imperial history.

    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    Why did the Spanish-American War begin so quickly, and how did it transform the United States into an overseas imperial power?

    Key Terms

    Jose Marti
    yellow journalism
    USS Maine
    Manila Bay
    San Juan Hill
    Rough Riders
    Treaty of Paris
    imperialism

    What to Watch For

    1. How Cuban resistance to Spain and American sympathy for that struggle set the stage for intervention.
    2. How yellow journalism and the USS Maine pushed public opinion toward war in 1898.
    3. How fighting in both Cuba and the Philippines made this more than just a Caribbean conflict.
    4. How victory over Spain created a fierce debate over whether the United States was liberating people or building an empire.

    Study Tip

    Track the lesson in three moves: causes of war, major victories, and the argument over empire afterward. That sequence makes the whole story much easier to remember.
