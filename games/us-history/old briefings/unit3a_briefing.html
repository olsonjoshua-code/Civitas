Unit 3A: Expansion and Global Power

Use these briefing slides to study how the United States shifted from older traditions of foreign policy toward overseas expansion, war with Spain, and a larger role in world affairs at the turn of the twentieth century.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores how American foreign policy evolved from neutrality and continental expansion toward imperial ambition, overseas war, and global intervention. Students trace how debates over realism, idealism, national interest, and American power shaped U.S. actions abroad.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How and why did the United States move from a more limited foreign policy to a more aggressive role as an imperial and global power?

What to Notice

  • How realism and idealism both shaped U.S. foreign policy.
  • How expansion overseas was tied to trade, military strategy, and national pride.
  • How the Spanish-American War became a turning point in America’s role in the world.
  • How Roosevelt, Taft, and Wilson used different approaches to intervention and power.

Study Tip

Track the shift across the unit: first the United States developed ideas about foreign policy, then it fought a war that expanded its reach, and finally it acted more directly around the world. Follow that progression from principles to war to global power.
