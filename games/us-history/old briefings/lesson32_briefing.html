    Study Guide

    Essential Question

    How did ordinary Americans endure the hardships of the Great Depression, and what did the experience reveal about inequality, resilience, and the limits of private charity?

    Key Terms

    black blizzard
    breadline
    Dust Bowl
    Great Flood of 1936
    Okies
    soup kitchen
    Hoovervilles
    The Grapes of Wrath

    What to Watch For

    1. How unemployment spread unevenly, hitting minorities, women, and the young hardest of all.
    2. How losing a job could cascade into losing a home, food security, and family stability.
    3. How the Dust Bowl combined economic collapse with environmental disaster to displace hundreds of thousands.
    4. How private charity, local government, and even individual ingenuity all proved insufficient against the scale of the crisis.

    Study Tip

    Keep two questions in mind for every slide: What did this person or group lose? And what did they do about it? The Depression story is both a story of suffering and a story of stubborn endurance.
