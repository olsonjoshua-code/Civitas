Interpreting Political Data
THE PROMPT
A political science researcher conducts a national poll and reports: "62% of Americans support a new federal healthcare program." The poll sampled 450 adults recruited through an online survey platform. The margin of error is reported as ±4.5 percentage points. A senator cites this poll to argue that Congress must pass the program immediately. Use the scenario above to answer the following:

(A) Identify one methodological limitation of the poll described in the scenario.
(B) Explain how the limitation you identified in Part A could affect the validity of the poll's findings and the senator's argument.
(C) Explain how political ideology could cause two different senators to interpret the same polling data in opposite ways.
